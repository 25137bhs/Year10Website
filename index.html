Why I Chose Digitech Again

I decided to take Digital Technology again this year because I absolutely loved the wide variety of topics we get to explore. From creating digital art and editing images to diving deep into the logic of computer science and writing my own Python programs, there's always something new and exciting to learn. It keeps me challenged and lets me be creative at the same time!

Image Editing

Vector Graphic Creation

In the image editing unit, we explored the foundations of digital art and design by learning how to manipulate layers, shapes, and color spaces. We focused on vector-style image creation to build clean graphics from the ground up, combining simple geometric elements to construct complex scenes. This hands-on process taught me how to effectively balance a visual layout, utilize depth and perspective, and select cohesive color palettes to bring digital concepts to life.

Computer Science

Computer Science Summary

In the first half of the computer science topic we dwelled into the fundamental ways computers translate and protect information. We explored how data is encoded through systems like barcodes and QR codes. A focus was also placed on data integrity and error detection; we studied how parity bits and check digits act as a first line of defense against data corruption.

About My Python Projects

In the second half of our computer science studies, we transitioned from theory to practical programming by diving into Grok's Python for Beginners course. Throughout this unit, I learned how to read, write, and debug code from scratch. We started by understanding data types like strings and integers, and quickly moved on to building logic using variables, user inputs, and conditional statements.

3D Modeling

My First 3D Interactive Model

For this unit, I learned how to use Blender to create a 3D model from scratch. I built this custom sign by shaping geometry, applying materials, and adjusting the emission strength to give the text a glowing LED effect.

Instead of just showing a picture, I used HTML and Google's model-viewer component to make the model fully interactive right here on the web. Go ahead and click, drag to spin it around, or scroll to zoom in!
